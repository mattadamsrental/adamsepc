What is an EPC?

An Energy Performance Certificate contains information about how energy is used in a home or a business premises, along with details of how much the energy used actually costs.

The assessment gives a property an energy efficiency rating from A (most efficient) to G (least efficient) and is valid for 10 years.

We can provide an EPC for properties with a no fuss and convenient service.

Do I need One?

If your are selling or renting your property you must have an EPC before the property goes on the market as this is a legal requirement in England and Wales.

Even if you are not marketing your property, and energy assessment can be a useful insight into your energy consumption and could give ideas on how to improve the efficiency of your property and reduce energy bills.

Preparing for your property's assessment

Because your home cannot fail an Energy Performance Certificate, there is no right way or wrong way to present your home for the EPC. You can however improve the results of the assessment before we arrive.

Why? Well by increasing this final result, you are not only saving money on energy bills, but demonstrating to your potential buyer or tenant that you are serious about saving energy and the more work you put in now, the better the results, the more likely you are to secure your buyer or tenant.

Any upgrade you can do now will pay dividends to your results. Just by replacing your light bulbs with LED bulbs will increase your energy performance rating, upgrading the jacket on your hot water cylinder will increase the results. Laying deeper insulation in the roof will help also.

Remember, that the more you can do to promote your home in a good light, the less opportunity a buyer or tenant has to negotiate the price!

Energy Performance Certificates

An EPC is needed whenever a property is:

  • built
  • sold
  • rented

You must order one for potential buyers and tenants before you market your property to sell or rent.

An EPC contains:

  • information about your property’s energy use and typical energy costs
  • recommendations about how to reduce energy use and save money
  • an energy efficiency rating from A (most efficient) to G (least efficient) and is valid for 10 years.
